Art Goals Pool

Status: approved by the Art instructor (July 2026). Distilled from the previous module's Art-Rubrics (M1–M3) and the New Preliminary Design timeline. The base pool below is Games-flavored; the Category adaptations section covers all 9 categories. Companion to the Design Goals Pool and the Team Formation Guide.

How Art goals work

Each team has 0–2 Art students (headcount confirmed at team formation, justified by the project's art surface). Like the Design track: each Art student takes 2 deliverable-sized goals per milestone (≈ one CS goal in effort). The first artist owns the common goal A1 ⭐ plus 1 pool pick; a second artist takes 2 more picks (all picks with instructor approval).

Teams with no Art student do not use this pool — the Design track's conditional goals (Design M1 #11 Art Proof of Concept, M2 #11 Art Implementation Quality) cover the minimum art surface instead.

Sizing and grading: same model as Design — goals are sized by deliverable + quality tier, each carrying a Quality ladder (Base → Good → Exceptional) distilled from the old rubric tiers. Base = "Done"; upper rungs raise the grade, not the deadline. Grading scale: Done / Partially Done / Missing.

AI policy (from the old rubrics): AI may be used for ideation and reference only — every AI-generated image must be annotated with the tool used; AI-generated assets may NOT ship in the prototype or product. All shipped art is team-made.

Cross-track dependencies: Art consumes the Design track's theme/GDD (D1 ⭐) and style decisions, and delivers through the CS art pipeline (data-driven asset loading, spritesheets, ≤4k texture resolution). The art document (working file in PowerPoint, submitted as PDF) is a living deliverable across both milestones — updates and new work must be clearly indicated each milestone.

Numbering: A1 ⭐ = common; #2, #3, … = pool picks. Pools are extensible — instructor-approved goals from new proposals are appended at the end.

Art — Games

Milestone 1 (Research, early concept ideation and mood boards)

  1. ⭐ Art Bible / Proof of Concept — the game's complete visual direction in one professional document.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Art POC document (pptx working file → PDF): content page, moodboards, art direction and style guide, write-ups for game concept, characters, environments, props, UI and VFX (as applicable), and at least 2 in-game mockup screens (typically main menu + in-game) that clearly indicate art direction and gameplay.
    • Prerequisites: Design D1 ⭐ (theme, genre, core concept).
    • Feeds into: Every other Art goal; Design M2 #2 (branding); CS art pipeline goals; the M1 "first-pass art doc" checkpoint (wk9).
    • Quality ladder: Base = complete per submission guidelines, organized, no missing materials. Good = additional mockup screens, clear descriptions, consistent and aesthetically coherent direction. Exceptional = professionally presented, high-quality assets, strong visual appeal throughout.
  2. Character Concepts & Model Sheets — the cast designed, researched and ready for production.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Research and conceptual designs for characters; main character + at least 1 enemy or NPC completed with solid anatomy and draftsmanship; model sheets and character studies (key poses, expressions).
    • Prerequisites: A1 ⭐ (art direction), Design M1 #8 (world/character bible) if picked.
    • Feeds into: M2 #2 (animation), M2 A1 ⭐ (production); the design prototype's placeholder replacement.
    • Quality ladder: Base = sufficient research + main characters decently designed. Good = appealing, style-consistent designs with model sheets and studies. Exceptional = professional draftsmanship and consistency across the cast.
  3. Environment, Props & VFX Concepts — where the game lives: key environments, props and effect designs.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Research and concept designs for environments, props and VFX (as applicable); key environment clearly designed, rendered and presented with good draftsmanship.
    • Prerequisites: A1 ⭐ (art direction).
    • Feeds into: M2 #3 (living environment), M2 A1 ⭐ (production), Design M1 #6 (level design visuals).
    • Quality ladder: Base = key environment + research done. Good = additional environments; direction refined through thorough research; blends well with characters and UI. Exceptional = professional-quality environment art, props and VFX concepts.
  4. UI Art Concepts & Typography — the interface's visual identity: type, logo, HUD clarity.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: UI concept designs and research; typography and logo exploration; brand identity of team and game represented; HUD elements designed for clarity; all UI art consistent with the game's overall direction.
    • Prerequisites: A1 ⭐ (style guide), Design M1 #4 (wireframes to skin).
    • Feeds into: M2 #4 (UI states), Design M2 D1 ⭐ (final polished UI), Design M2 #2 (branding).
    • Quality ladder: Base = basic UI concepts + research. Good = designs synergize with game concepts; typography/logo/brand identity explored; HUD clarity. Exceptional = appealing, high-quality designs that fit the game.
  5. Animation Foundations — timing, key poses and the first cycles that prove the characters can move.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Animation plan for critical gameplay actions; key poses and timing tests; at least the main character's core cycle (e.g., idle/walk) demonstrating understanding of animation principles.
    • Prerequisites: M1 #2 (character designs) or A1 ⭐.
    • Feeds into: M2 #2 (extensive animation), CS animation system (spritesheet format).
    • Quality ladder: Base = plan + first core cycle. Good = cycles show character personality, not generic motion. Exceptional = strong command of animation principles across tests.
  6. Early Asset Production — Prototype Support — first-pass critical assets flowing into the prototype from week 8.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: First-pass critical sprites/tiles/UI elements delivered for the design prototype (production starts by wk8 per the module timeline), integrated at correct scale, consistent with the art bible.
    • Prerequisites: A1 ⭐ (direction), Design M1 #2 (the prototype to support).
    • Feeds into: M2 A1 ⭐ (production continues), the wk9 prototype checkpoint's visual credibility.
    • Quality ladder: Base = critical prototype assets delivered and integrated. Good = consistent style, correct scaling, readable gameplay. Exceptional = near-final quality already in the prototype.

Milestone 2 (Style Guide and Art direction)

  1. ⭐ Critical Asset Production & In-Engine Visual Quality — every critical asset completed, implemented and consistent in the showcase build.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: All critical art (characters, environments, props, UI) completed at minimum first-pass quality and implemented in the showcase build — no placeholders; correct scale (matching pixel sizes, no stretching/distortion); consistent art direction; the art document updated with all M2 work clearly indicated (and reasons noted if any asset is not yet in the engine).
    • Prerequisites: All M1 Art goals taken; CS art pipeline (data-driven asset loading).
    • Feeds into: The trimester showcase; sets the visual standard the T2 product must match; T2 asset production plan.
    • Quality ladder: Base = completion — acceptable quality, correct scale, minimum artifacts. Good = good draftsmanship, well-themed, strong consistency across all assets. Exceptional = high-quality art throughout, professional attention to detail.
  2. Character Animation — Extensive — the cast in motion: complete key animations, personality, breadth.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Key character/prop animations completed (main character + 1 enemy/NPC minimum) supporting all critical gameplay actions; player character animation extensive and lively, showing clear personality; implemented in the build.
    • Prerequisites: M1 #5 (foundations), M1 #2 (designs); CS animation system.
    • Feeds into: M2 A1 ⭐ (implementation quality), Design M2 #8 (game feel).
    • Quality ladder: Base = critical gameplay animations done, first-pass, glitch-free. Good = decent quality throughout; personality evident. Exceptional = high quality with strong animation principles; additional animation sets for other characters.
  3. Environment Alive — Ambient Animation & Layering — depth, light and motion that make the world breathe.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Environment implemented with foreground/background layering for depth, lighting mood, and ambient animation (moving clouds, swaying trees, water, dust, blinking lights, creatures).
    • Prerequisites: M1 #3 (environment concepts); CS rendering (layers).
    • Feeds into: M2 A1 ⭐; the showcase's perceived production value.
    • Quality ladder: Base = layered environment implemented, readable at all times. Good = several ambient animations create life. Exceptional = a living world — environment art as a signature of the game.
  4. UI Art — States & Feedback — complete interface art with living states, policy-compliant type and themed menus.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: All critical UI (menus, HUD) art complete — no WIP or placeholders; fonts appropriate and policy-compliant (max 3: headings, in-game, small text); text clean, readable, well-aligned; visual feedback states (press/hover/on-off, glows, size changes) implemented; menus and HUD themed to the game.
    • Prerequisites: M1 #4 (UI concepts), Design M1 #4 (flows); CS GUI capability.
    • Feeds into: Design M2 D1 ⭐ (final polished UI), Design M2 #3 (motion design).
    • Quality ladder: Base = critical UI complete with compliant fonts and clean text. Good = feedback states implemented; clarity and intuitive navigation. Exceptional = high-quality themed menus/HUD with animated flair.
  5. VFX — Critical Effects & Transitions — the effects that sell gameplay moments, plus transitions with style.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: All VFX critical to gameplay at first-pass quality (impacts, combat moments, pickups, respawns), of reasonable artistic quality without glitches; menu/HUD VFX considered.
    • Prerequisites: M1 #3 (VFX concepts); CS particle/graphics goals.
    • Feeds into: Design M2 #8 (juice pass), M2 A1 ⭐.
    • Quality ladder: Base = critical VFX present, decent, glitch-free. Good = high-quality VFX showing animation principles. Exceptional = interesting VFX transitions (levels, respawns, impacts) — Persona 5-style motion identity.
  6. Cutscenes & Storyboards — the narrative visualized: from thumbnails to rendered scenes.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Cutscene planning and production: research, thumbnail sketches, storyboards, color scripts, progressing to rendered first-pass cutscene art for the scenes the game needs (waivable if the game has no cutscenes).
    • Prerequisites: Design M1 #8 (narrative beats), M1 #2 (characters).
    • Feeds into: Design M2 #9 (narrative presentation), the showcase's storytelling.
    • Quality ladder: Base = storyboards for all planned scenes. Good = partial scenes rendered. Exceptional = all cutscene art rendered at first-pass quality.
  7. Additional Asset Expansion — beyond the critical set: more levels, characters, props, UI variants.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: A defined expansion set beyond the critical assets — additional level/segment art, NPCs, props, UI variants or VFX — with research/concepts where new ground is broken; only attempted once critical assets are complete.
    • Prerequisites: M2 A1 ⭐ well underway (critical set first).
    • Feeds into: T2 production head start; showcase breadth.
    • Quality ladder: Base = expansion set delivered, consistent with direction. Good = good draftsmanship and consistency throughout. Exceptional = significant quantity at high quality.

Category adaptations

Pattern approved by the Art instructor (Education, July 2026); the subsections follow the approved model. A1 ⭐'s arc (art bible/POC → critical asset production & in-engine quality) and the UI-art, animation and asset-expansion picks are universal. The subsections below give the category readings, plus surface & headcount guidance (the team-formation rule: the art surface justifies the slot).

Education + Training

  • Surface & headcount: mid-to-high for visual learning products (mascots, exercise illustrations, scene art) — 1 artist typical, 2 justified for character/animation-heavy learning games; 0 acceptable for text/audio-centric trainers (typing tutor, ear training), covered by the Design conditionals.
  • A1 ⭐ (M1) reads as the learning product's visual language: age-appropriate style, mascot/instructor character direction, iconography rules; the 2 mockup screens = 2 exercise screens. A1 ⭐ (M2): critical assets = the exercise scenes, mascot set and feedback visuals used by the showcase lesson unit.
  • M1 #2 Character Concepts → mascot / instructor characters (expressions sheet: neutral, encouraging, corrective).
  • M1 #3 Environment, Props & VFX Concepts → exercise scenes & item illustrations (the objects lessons manipulate).
  • M1 #5 Animation Foundations → feedback & mascot animation (correct/incorrect reactions, idle life).
  • M2 #2 Character Animation → mascot reaction set integrated with the Design feedback pass.
  • M2 #3 Environment Alive → lively exercise scenes (ambient motion that doesn't distract from the task).
  • M2 #5 VFX → celebration & progress effects (streaks, level-ups, completion).
  • M2 #6 Cutscenes & Storyboards → tutorial / story sequences framing lesson units.

Simulator

  • Surface & headcount: low-to-mid (world stylization, agent sprites, data-viz styling) — 0–1 artist; 0 common (Design conditionals cover it). 1 justified for stylized, watchable sims.
  • A1 ⭐: visual language of the simulated world + dashboard/report styling; mockups = live view + report screen.
  • M1 #2 Characters → agent/vehicle/entity sprites. #3 Environment → world tiles, maps, zone styling. #5 Animation → agent motion cycles.
  • M2 #2 → agent animation sets · #3 → living-world ambience · #5 → state-change VFX (alerts, events) · #6 → intro/tour storyboards.

Fintech

  • Surface & headcount: low — 0–1 artist; 0 typical. 1 justified only for brand/illustration-led products.
  • A1 ⭐: brand-led visual language + data-viz styling; mockups = dashboard + a mobile action flow.
  • M1 #2 Characters → optional mascot / empty-state illustration system. #3 Environment → iconography set. #5 Animation → micro-animation foundations.
  • M2 #2 → illustration animation · #3 → subtle dashboard ambience · #5 → transaction/alert effects · #6 → onboarding storyboards.

GovTech

  • Surface & headcount: low — 0–1 artist; 0 typical. 1 justified for illustration-led civic products.
  • A1 ⭐: civic design language — accessible palette, iconography, map styling; mockups = citizen screen + staff dashboard.
  • M1 #2 Characters → people/services illustration system. #3 Environment → map styling & icon sets. #5 Animation → micro-animations.
  • M2 #5 → status-change effects · #6 → explainer storyboards · others as per reading above.

Social Media

  • Surface & headcount: mid — 1 artist typical; 2 for content-presentation-heavy products.
  • A1 ⭐: brand + content-presentation language; mockups = feed + profile.
  • M1 #2 Characters → avatar/sticker set. #3 Environment → canvas/background/room art. #5 Animation → reaction animations.
  • M2 #2 → animated stickers/reactions · #3 → lively shared spaces · #5 → celebration VFX · #6 → onboarding story sequences.

CMS

  • Surface & headcount: low-to-mid — 0–1 artist. 1 justified for theme-led products.
  • A1 ⭐: theme design language (typography-forward); mockups = editor + published page.
  • M1 #2 Characters → template illustration set. #3 Environment → theme art & backgrounds. #5 Animation → editor micro-animations.
  • M2 #3 → theme gallery variety · #5 → editor transition effects · #6 → template preview storyboards.

Immersive Experience

  • Surface & headcount: high — rivals Games: 1–2 artists; 0 only for purely generative pieces (Design conditionals then apply).
  • A1 ⭐, #2, #3, #5 and all M2 picks apply essentially as written — characters, environments, animation and cutscenes are native here; the "game" is the experience.

Creative Tools & Media Production

  • Surface & headcount: mid — 0–1 artist: the tool's chrome plus its demo content. 1 justified when the showcase needs strong made-with-it content.
  • A1 ⭐: the tool's visual identity + art direction for demo content; mockups = workspace + export/preview screen.
  • M1 #2 Characters → demo-content characters/assets. #3 Environment → demo scenes & sample packs. #5 Animation → UI/preview animations.
  • M2 #6 → tutorial storyboards · #7 → sample content packs shipped with the tool.
