Team Formation Guide

Status: approved (July 2026). Companion to the Design Goals Pool and the Art Goals Pool. This page is the single source of truth for team composition rules — other documents link here rather than restating them.

Hard rules

  • Every team has 6 CS students (fixed).
  • Every team adds 1–2 Design students — at least 1 Design student is mandated.
  • Every team may add 0–2 Art students.
  • Maximum team size: 9. So Design + Art ≤ 3: picking 1 Designer allows up to 2 Artists; picking 2 Designers allows up to 1 Artist.
  • Additions depend on the nature of the chosen project and require instructor approval (the Art headcount is confirmed at team formation, subject to justification of project complexity/type).
  • The instructors of each track (CS, Design, Art) remain the approval gates for their students' goals.

Composition patterns and trade-offs

Composition (team size) Best for Trade-offs to weigh
1D + 1A (8) The balanced default for most game projects. One person per discipline — no redundancy; Design covers UX + game design alone.
1D + 2A (9) Art-heavy games: rich environments, character animation, heavy VFX. Strong visuals, but a solo designer carries GDD, prototype and validation; art direction must be agreed between 2 artists.
2D + 1A (9) Systems- and content-heavy games: deep UX, complex economies, lots of levels/missions. Full Design coverage (up to 6 goals/milestone) with dedicated art; art breadth is limited to what 1 artist can produce.
2D + 0A (8) UX-heavy products with lean visual needs; teams confident in a strong visual-design skillset within Design. No dedicated artist: the (conditional) Art POC / Art Implementation goals become mandatory Design picks — cleanly covered with 2 designers.
1D + 0A (7) Visually lean projects only (minimal art surface). Riskiest mix: the solo designer's picks are forced to the conditional Art goals, and the M1 prototype drops to a core-loop sketch inside D1 ⭐ (see the conditional rule in the Design Goals Pool). Requires explicit instructor sign-off.

Rule of thumb: count the project's art surface (characters, environments, animation, VFX) and design surface (systems depth, UX breadth, content volume) at pitch time — the bigger surface gets the second slot.

Goal load per track (per milestone)

  • CS: 6 goals — one ~40 h goal per student (G1–G3 ⭐ common + 3 pool picks).
  • Design: up to 3 goals per Design student — commons D1 ⭐ + TU1 ⭐ (the UI goals, migrated from TEAM July 15 and graded by the UI instructor under Design) + picks, deliverable-sized with quality ladders (three Design goals ≈ 1.5 CS goals in effort).
  • Art: 2 goals per Art student — A1 ⭐ + picks, deliverable-sized with quality ladders (see the Art Goals Pool).
  • TEAM goals: TA1 ⭐ (Audio) each milestone + TP1 ⭐ (Final Presentation, M2 only — 7-minute class presentation: custom tools + prototype demo + team post-mortem); mandatory for every team; shared grade affecting every member, coordinated by named champions (see TEAM Goals). (The UI goals TU1 ⭐ live in the Design track since July 15.)

Formation timeline

Week What happens
1 Profiling quiz (aspirations, interests, competencies). Job-listing "shapes" given; every student submits the listing they apply for.
1–2 CS: specialization prototype (bootcamp). Design/Art: portfolio updated for the chosen listing.
3 Teams formed. Art headcount confirmed (0–2, justified by project complexity/type). Design: user research starts.
4 Product pitch (conversational, per team — not a big presentation). Proposal assessed against the 9 categories.
5 Milestone goals confirmed per track; project kickoff.

All four tracks share one goal workbook per milestone (sheets: Team Info · CS Goals · Design Goals · Art Goals · TEAM Goals), kept on OneDrive so the whole team sees each other's goals and co-authors without file locks. Sheet protection locks the structure (banners, headers, notes); data cells stay editable, and each instructor grades only their own sheet (Status + Instructor Feedback).

Goals are graded per milestone as Done / Partially Done / Missing by the owning track's instructor — the same scale for all tracks (CS, Design, Art, and TEAM goals). Exception inside Design: TU1 ⭐ is graded by the UI instructor (grade affects the Design students only).

Per-category composition guidance

Complete for all 9 categories (pattern approved via Education, July 2026). The hard rules above always apply; this table records the typical composition per category and when the optional slots are justified.

Category Typical composition Notes
Games 6 CS + 1–2 D + 1–2 A (≤9) Full art surface; second designer or second artist per the pattern trade-offs above.
Education + Training 6 CS + 1 D + 1 A Mascot/illustration surface justifies 1 artist; 2 for character-heavy learning games. Text/audio-centric trainers (typing, ear training) may run 0 A + 2 D with the Design conditionals.
Simulator 6 CS + 2 D + 0 A Scenario & data-viz design surface; artists rare — 1 only for stylized, watchable sims.
Fintech 6 CS + 2 D + 0 A Trust/clarity design heavy; 1 artist only for brand/illustration-led products.
GovTech 6 CS + 2 D + 0 A Service design heavy (two roles); 1 artist for illustration-led civic products.
Social Media 6 CS + 1–2 D + 0–1 A Brand & content presentation justify 1 artist.
CMS 6 CS + 2 D + 0–1 A Template/workflow design heavy; 1 artist for theme-led products.
Immersive Experience 6 CS + 1 D + 2 A (or 2 D + 1 A) Art surface rivals Games; 0 artists only for purely generative pieces.
Creative Tools & Media Production 6 CS + 1–2 D + 0–1 A Tool-UX design heavy; 1 artist for tool chrome + made-with-it demo content.
Visualization & Analytics 6 CS + 2 D + 0–1 A Data-UX design heavy; artists rare — 1 for brand-led personal/sports products.
