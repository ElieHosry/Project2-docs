How TEAM goals work

TEAM goals are cross-disciplinary goals owned by the whole team. Every team carries TA1 ⭐ (Audio) in each milestone, plus TP1 ⭐ (Final Presentation) in M2 — both mandatory (only an instructor can waive TA1 ⭐, e.g. for a deliberately soundless product; TP1 ⭐ is never waived).

Champion: the team names one champion per TEAM goal (any track) who coordinates it. Coordination happens inside the champion's existing goals' scope; the underlying work is distributed across track goals (the CS audio system, Design direction, Art SFX-supporting visuals).

Grading: the old module's rule is preserved — a TEAM goal's grade affects every member. TA1 ⭐ is graded by the Audio instructor (floating across teams); TP1 ⭐ is graded jointly by the attending instructors at the end-of-trimester session. Scale: Done / Partially Done / Missing, with quality ladders as in the Design/Art pools. (The UI instructor now grades TU1 ⭐ inside the Design track.)

Numbering & extensibility: TA1 ⭐ (Audio) and TP1 ⭐ (Presentation) are the mandatory TEAM goals (TA per milestone, TP in M2). The pools are extensible — future instructor-approved goals would join as TA2/TP2, … optional picks.

Origin: the audio goals migrated here from the Design pool (its former picks M1 D9 / M2 D10), so a baseline of audio and UI quality is guaranteed in EVERY project — not only in teams that happened to pick those goals. The Design pool was renumbered accordingly.

TEAM — Games

Milestone 1 (Research phase)

  • TA1 ⭐ Audio Direction & Plan — the game's sound identity on paper, with a plan the team can actually execute.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Audio concept document: theme and direction, full BGM/SFX needs list, at least 4 audio/video references, priority order for M2 integration, a sourcing plan (who produces or sources what, with license checks) and an integration plan against the CS audio system.
    • Prerequisites: Design D1 ⭐ (theme/genre).
    • Feeds into: M2 TA1 ⭐; the CS audio system goals; Art VFX timing where audio-visual sync matters.
    • Quality ladder: Base = complete list + references + sourcing plan. Good = direction specific enough to brief a composer, mapped to the CS audio goals. Exceptional = produced concept audio or an interactive audio sketch.

Milestone 2 (Iterated Prototype phase)

  • TA1 ⭐ Audio Integration & Quality — at least 1 BGM and 6 distinct SFX types, integrated, themed and clean in the showcase build.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: ≥1 BGM and ≥6 distinct SFX types (different sounds, not variations of one) integrated via the CS audio system, fitting the theme and gameplay; files clean and well-edited; loops smooth; no cutoffs (engine supports multiple channels). Graded from a gameplay recording with good-quality audio and no narration. Rhythm games: audio, visuals and input must be in sync (clause waived for non-rhythm games).
    • Prerequisites: M1 TA1 ⭐ (direction and plan); the CS audio system.
    • Feeds into: Design M2 D8 (the audio half of game feel); the showcase recording quality.
    • Quality ladder: Base = required set integrated and working. Good = good quality, smooth loops, fits the theme well. Exceptional = original BGM and/or SFX tailor-made by team members.
  • TP1 ⭐ Final Presentation (M2 only — uniform for all categories) — 7 minutes in front of the class and the instructors: what you built, how you built it, what you learned.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: A 7-minute team presentation at the end-of-trimester session (wk14, teams grouped by product genre): a live showcase of the project's custom tools (the editor/authoring pipeline), a live demo of the prototype on the showcase build, and a team post-mortem (what went well, what went wrong, what the team would do differently — across tracks). Hard 7-minute limit.
    • Prerequisites: The M2 showcase build; CS M2 G2 ⭐ (the tools to demo); every track's M2 outputs.
    • Feeds into: T2 planning (the post-mortem's actions); the module showcase.
    • Quality ladder: Base = within time; tools and prototype demoed live; post-mortem covers well/wrong/lessons. Good = rehearsed handoffs, every track represented, demo runs without stalls. Exceptional = a compelling narrative and an honest, actionable post-mortem that names causes, not symptoms.

Category calibrations

Pattern approved by the Audio and UI instructors (Education, July 2026); the subsections follow the approved model. TA1 ⭐'s floor is calibrated per category (the Games floor of ≥1 BGM + ≥6 SFX types stays the default where unstated). The former TU1 ⭐ screen calibrations now live in the Design Goals Pool's Category adaptations.

Education + Training

  • TA1 ⭐ (M2) floor: ≥1 BGM (ambient/focus — must not distract from learning) + ≥6 SFX types, of which distinct correct / incorrect / progress cues are mandatory three. Products where audio is the content (ear training, rhythm exercises) treat TA1 ⭐ as load-bearing: the sync clause applies, coordinated with the CS audio goals.

Simulator

  • TA1 ⭐ (M2) floor: BGM optional (ambient bed, waivable); ≥5 distinct event cues — run start/stop, alert, state change, completion — non-intrusive, control-room legible.

Fintech

  • TA1 ⭐ (M2) floor: no BGM required; ≥5 distinct feedback cues (confirm, error, alert, success, notification), restrained and professional.

GovTech

  • TA1 ⭐ (M2) floor: no BGM; ≥5 cues (submission, status change, alert, error, success); every audio cue paired with a visual equivalent (accessibility).

Social Media

  • TA1 ⭐ (M2) floor: ≥6 cues incl. notification, message, reaction, error; respectful defaults (mute available, no notification spam).

CMS

  • TA1 ⭐ (M2) floor: editor: ≥5 subtle cues (save, publish, error, undo, notification); published sites are silent by default.

Immersive Experience

  • TA1 ⭐ (M2) floor: the full Games floor applies (≥1 BGM + ≥6 SFX types) and meets the category's Spatial/Reactive Audio purple requirement; the sync clause applies to audio-reactive pieces.

Creative Tools & Media Production

  • TA1 ⭐ (M2) floor: ≥5 tool feedback cues (action confirm, error, export complete, undo, alert); for audio products (DAW, tracker) TA1 ⭐ is load-bearing and the sync clause applies.
