TEAM Goals — Audio & UI

Status: approved by the Audio and UI instructors (July 2026) — Games-scoped. Distilled from the previous module's team-level Audio and UI rubrics (Designers-Rubrics M2–M3). Companion to the CS goal pools, the Design Goals Pool, the Art Goals Pool and the Team Formation Guide.

How TEAM goals work

TEAM goals are cross-disciplinary goals owned by the whole team — audio and UI quality are properties of the integrated product, not of any single track. Every team carries 2 TEAM goals per milestone: TA ⭐ (Audio) and TU ⭐ (UI). They are mandatory; only an instructor can waive one (e.g., a deliberately soundless game).

Champion: the team names one champion per TEAM goal (any track) who coordinates it. Coordination happens inside the champion's existing goals' scope; the underlying work is distributed across track goals (the CS audio system and GUI, Design flows and polish, Art UI skins and SFX-supporting visuals).

Grading: the old module's rule is preserved — a TEAM goal's grade affects every member. TA ⭐ is graded by the Audio instructor and TU ⭐ by the UI instructor (they float across teams), on the scale Done / Partially Done / Missing, with quality ladders as in the Design/Art pools.

Origin: the audio goals migrated here from the Design pool (its former picks M1 #9 / M2 #10), so a baseline of audio and UI quality is guaranteed in EVERY project — not only in teams that happened to pick those goals. The Design pool was renumbered accordingly.

TEAM — Games

Milestone 1 (Research phase)

  1. TA ⭐ Audio Direction & Plan — the game's sound identity on paper, with a plan the team can actually execute.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Audio concept document: theme and direction, full BGM/SFX needs list, at least 4 audio/video references, priority order for M2 integration, a sourcing plan (who produces or sources what, with license checks) and an integration plan against the CS audio system.
    • Prerequisites: Design D1 ⭐ (theme/genre).
    • Feeds into: M2 TA ⭐; the CS audio system goals; Art VFX timing where audio-visual sync matters.
    • Quality ladder: Base = complete list + references + sourcing plan. Good = direction specific enough to brief a composer, mapped to the CS audio goals. Exceptional = produced concept audio or an interactive audio sketch.
  2. TU ⭐ UI Standards & Wireframe Baseline — the minimum interface bar every team clears before production.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: Team-wide UI baseline: complete screen-flow map, control mapping to industry standards (touch-first for the mobile target), readability rules adopted (fonts, contrast, touch-target sizes), and a wireframe review session with the UI instructor.
    • Prerequisites: Design D1 ⭐; builds on Design M1 #4 / #10 where those are picked — but the baseline is due from every team regardless of picks. Where Design #4 is picked, the TU review assesses those wireframes — no separate artifact is expected (one flow map serves both goals).
    • Feeds into: M2 TU ⭐; Design M2 D1 ⭐ (final polished UI).
    • Quality ladder: Base = flows complete + standards adopted. Good = UI-instructor feedback incorporated and documented. Exceptional = wireframes usability-tested within M1.

Milestone 2 (Iterated Prototype phase)

  1. TA ⭐ Audio Integration & Quality — at least 1 BGM and 6 distinct SFX types, integrated, themed and clean in the showcase build.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: ≥1 BGM and ≥6 distinct SFX types (different sounds, not variations of one) integrated via the CS audio system, fitting the theme and gameplay; files clean and well-edited; loops smooth; no cutoffs (engine supports multiple channels). Graded from a gameplay recording with good-quality audio and no narration. Rhythm games: audio, visuals and input must be in sync (clause waived for non-rhythm games).
    • Prerequisites: M1 TA ⭐ (direction and plan); the CS audio system.
    • Feeds into: Design M2 #8 (the audio half of game feel); the showcase recording quality.
    • Quality ladder: Base = required set integrated and working. Good = good quality, smooth loops, fits the theme well. Exceptional = original BGM and/or SFX tailor-made by team members.
  2. TU ⭐ Functional UI & Usability — every menu works, on the mobile target, for someone who has never seen the game.
    Details & dependencies
    • Deliverable: In the showcase build on the mobile target: all menus function — Start, Pause (game actually pauses; cycle back to main menu available), "Resume Game" (exact label), "Quit Game" (exact label, with confirmation), "How to Play" screen accessible from the pause menu; touch equivalents replace PC-only conventions; critical HUD present and readable; first-time users can navigate unaided.
    • Prerequisites: M1 TU ⭐; the Design M2 D1 ⭐ work; CS GUI capability.
    • Feeds into: The showcase demo; the T2 product's UI standard.
    • Quality ladder: Base = all menus functional with standard labels and flows. Good = intuitive, clear layouts with evident feedback states. Exceptional = modern animated motion UI validated with first-time users.

Category calibrations

Pattern approved by the Audio and UI instructors (Education, July 2026); the subsections follow the approved model. TU ⭐ is near-universal — only the M2 required-screens list varies. TA ⭐'s floor is calibrated per category (the Games floor of ≥1 BGM + ≥6 SFX types stays the default where unstated).

Education + Training

  • TA ⭐ (M2) floor: ≥1 BGM (ambient/focus — must not distract from learning) + ≥6 SFX types, of which distinct correct / incorrect / progress cues are mandatory three. Products where audio is the content (ear training, rhythm exercises) treat TA ⭐ as load-bearing: the sync clause applies, coordinated with the CS audio goals.
  • TU ⭐ (M2) required screens/states: home / lesson select · in-exercise with pause · Resume and Quit (exact labels, quit with confirmation) · results/progress screen · settings · how-to-play/help · separate teacher and learner entry points where the product has both roles.

Simulator

  • TA ⭐ (M2) floor: BGM optional (ambient bed, waivable); ≥5 distinct event cues — run start/stop, alert, state change, completion — non-intrusive, control-room legible.
  • TU ⭐ (M2) screens: home/scenario select · run view with pause & speed controls · results/report · settings · help. Resume/Quit labels apply to run sessions.

Fintech

  • TA ⭐ (M2) floor: no BGM required; ≥5 distinct feedback cues (confirm, error, alert, success, notification), restrained and professional.
  • TU ⭐ (M2) screens: home/dashboard · action flow (e.g. place order / add expense) with confirmation on anything money-like · history/reports · settings · help.

GovTech

  • TA ⭐ (M2) floor: no BGM; ≥5 cues (submission, status change, alert, error, success); every audio cue paired with a visual equivalent (accessibility).
  • TU ⭐ (M2) screens: citizen home · submit flow · status tracking · staff queue/dashboard view · settings · help — both roles navigable unaided.

Social Media

  • TA ⭐ (M2) floor: ≥6 cues incl. notification, message, reaction, error; respectful defaults (mute available, no notification spam).
  • TU ⭐ (M2) screens: feed/home · composer · notifications · profile · settings · help — with designed empty states for a fresh account.

CMS

  • TA ⭐ (M2) floor: editor: ≥5 subtle cues (save, publish, error, undo, notification); published sites are silent by default.
  • TU ⭐ (M2) screens: document list/home · editing canvas · preview toggle · publish flow with confirmation · settings · help.

Immersive Experience

  • TA ⭐ (M2) floor: the full Games floor applies (≥1 BGM + ≥6 SFX types) and meets the category's Spatial/Reactive Audio purple requirement; the sync clause applies to audio-reactive pieces.
  • TU ⭐ (M2) screens: minimal, diegetic-first UI: entry/attract screen · pause/exit · settings (volume, accessibility) · instructions — UI must not break immersion.

Creative Tools & Media Production

  • TA ⭐ (M2) floor: ≥5 tool feedback cues (action confirm, error, export complete, undo, alert); for audio products (DAW, tracker) TA ⭐ is load-bearing and the sync clause applies.
  • TU ⭐ (M2) screens: home/document manager · main editing workspace · export flow · settings incl. shortcuts · help/tutorial; the Quit-with-confirmation rule becomes an unsaved-changes guard.
